Activity #1

Vector graphics are composed of paths defined by mathematical equations, allowing for infinite scaling without quality loss, making them ideal for logos and illustrations. In contrast, raster graphics consist of pixels and can become pixelated when resized, making them better suited for photographs. In summary, vectors are scalable and resolution-independent, while rasters are pixel-based and resolution-dependent.

Enhancing photos involves improving their visual quality through techniques like color correction, sharpening, and cropping. This process may include applying filters, retouching blemishes, reducing noise, and creating depth-of-field effects. Overall, photo enhancement aims to make images more vibrant and appealing using various editing tools.

Activity #4

A pixelated character is a visual representation of a character in a digital format that uses small squares, or "pixels," to create the image. This style is often associated with retro video games and 8-bit or 16-bit graphics, where the limited resolution and color palette give the characters a blocky and simplistic appearance. Pixel art can evoke nostalgia and is still popular in indie games and art.
